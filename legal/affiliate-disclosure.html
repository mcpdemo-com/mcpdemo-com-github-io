01 FTC Compliance Statement

In accordance with the Federal Trade Commission's Guides Concerning the Use of Endorsements and Testimonials in Advertising (16 CFR Part 255) and the FTC's Disclosures: How to Make Effective Disclosures in Digital Advertising, mcpdemo.com discloses all material connections between the site and any third parties whose products or services are mentioned, reviewed, linked to, or demonstrated.

This disclosure applies to all content published on mcpdemo.com, including but not limited to: blog posts, tool information pages, demonstration pages, comparison articles, and any content distributed via email newsletter or social media channels operated by MCP Demo.

Effinsoftware.com, INC., the operator of mcpdemo.com, takes FTC compliance seriously. If you have questions about a specific relationship or endorsement not addressed below, please contact us.

02 What Is an Affiliate Relationship?

An affiliate (or referral) relationship exists when mcpdemo.com earns compensation — typically a commission, referral fee, or other benefit — when you click a link to a third-party product or service and subsequently take an action, such as making a purchase, signing up for a free trial, or registering for a service.

These relationships allow us to earn revenue that helps fund the operation of this website and the creation of free educational content about MCP tools and AI integrations. The existence of an affiliate relationship does not mean that a product or service is endorsed as the best option for every visitor — it means we have a financial relationship with that company.

Material connections we may have include:

  • Affiliate commission programs (pay-per-sale or pay-per-lead);
  • Referral programs that provide account credits or other non-cash benefits;
  • Sponsored content or paid placements (clearly labeled as such);
  • Free product access or trial accounts provided by vendors for review or demonstration purposes;
  • Reciprocal promotional arrangements.

03 How We Identify Affiliate Links

We strive to make affiliate relationships clear and easy to identify. We use the following conventions:

  • Affiliate and referral links may be preceded or followed by a disclosure such as "(affiliate link)", "(referral link)", or "[Ad]";
  • Pages or sections containing multiple affiliate links will include a disclosure notice at the top;
  • This Affiliate Disclosure page itself serves as a site-wide notice that affiliate relationships may exist throughout the content.

Where affiliate links are used within demonstration pages, we will clearly note that the featured tool may be linked via an affiliate relationship. This does not influence the functionality of the demonstration or the content of the information page.

Important: The presence of a link — with or without a disclosure tag — does not guarantee that the link is or is not affiliated. When in doubt, assume a link may be an affiliate link and refer to this disclosure page. If you require certainty about a specific link, contact us.

06 Editorial Independence

The existence of an affiliate relationship with a product or service provider does not mean that product will receive a favorable review, feature placement, or recommendation. Our editorial policy requires that:

  • Content accurately reflects our genuine assessment of tools and services;
  • We will disclose when a product has been provided for free for review or demonstration;
  • Affiliate compensation does not influence the outcome of reviews, comparisons, or feature selections;
  • We will not suppress negative information about affiliated products;
  • Sponsored or paid content will always be clearly labeled.

We may decline affiliate arrangements with companies whose products or practices conflict with our values or the interests of our audience.

07 No Additional Cost to You

When you use an affiliate link on mcpdemo.com, you pay the same price you would have paid without the affiliate link. In some cases, we may be able to offer exclusive discounts or extended trial periods through our affiliate relationships, which would benefit you.

Commissions and referral fees are paid to us by the merchant, not by you. They are a way for merchants to compensate publishers for driving traffic and conversions.
