01 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work, or work more efficiently, and to provide information to site owners.

Cookies can be:

  • Session cookies: Temporary files that are deleted when you close your browser.
  • Persistent cookies: Files that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them.
  • First-party cookies: Set directly by mcpdemo.com.
  • Third-party cookies: Set by third-party services that appear on our pages (e.g., Google Analytics, YouTube).

In addition to traditional cookies, we may also use similar tracking technologies such as web beacons (pixel tags), local storage, and session storage. This policy refers collectively to all such technologies as "cookies."

02 Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Keep the website functioning correctly (essential operations);
  • Understand how visitors use our site so we can improve it;
  • Remember your preferences and settings;
  • Enable embedded content from third parties such as YouTube;
  • Manage your session when logged in to an account;
  • Support affiliate and referral tracking.

03 Types of Cookies We Use

We categorize cookies as follows:

  • Strictly Necessary: Required for the site to function. Cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics & Performance: Help us understand visitor behavior. Require consent for EU/UK visitors.
  • Third-Party / Functional: Set by embedded services (YouTube). Activated when you interact with embedded content.
  • Marketing (Future): Not currently active. Will require consent before deployment.

05 Analytics Cookies

We use Google Analytics (provided by Google LLC) to collect anonymized information about how visitors use mcpdemo.com. This helps us understand which pages are popular, how users navigate the site, and where we can improve the experience.

Google Analytics collects information including:

  • Pages viewed and time spent on each page;
  • Approximate geographic location (country/city level, derived from IP address);
  • Browser type, device type, and operating system;
  • How you arrived at the site (search engine, direct, referral);
  • Interactions with interactive elements.

Cookie: _ga | Purpose: Distinguishes unique users | Duration: 2 years | Party: Google (Third-party)

Cookie: _ga_* | Purpose: Maintains session state | Duration: 2 years | Party: Google (Third-party)

Cookie: _gid | Purpose: Distinguishes users (24-hour) | Duration: 24 hours | Party: Google (Third-party)

We have configured Google Analytics to anonymize IP addresses. Data collected by Google Analytics is subject to Google's Privacy Policy.

You can opt out of Google Analytics across all websites using the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

06 Third-Party & Embedded Content

YouTube (Google LLC)

Some pages on mcpdemo.com embed YouTube videos. When you play or interact with an embedded YouTube video, YouTube may set cookies on your device to track your viewing preferences and behavior, even if you do not have a YouTube account.

Where possible, we use YouTube's privacy-enhanced mode (youtube-nocookie.com), which limits cookie placement until you choose to play a video. Once you play a video, YouTube's standard cookies apply.

YouTube cookies are governed by Google's Privacy Policy and YouTube's Terms of Service.

Affiliate & Referral Tracking

When you arrive at mcpdemo.com via an affiliate or referral link, a cookie may be set to attribute the visit to the referring partner. This cookie does not collect personal information and is used solely for commission tracking. See our Affiliate Disclosure for more details.

Cookie: ref_id | Purpose: Affiliate/referral tracking | Duration: 30 days | Party: First-party

09 Cookie Consent (EU/UK)

In compliance with the EU ePrivacy Directive ("Cookie Law") and GDPR, we will not place non-essential cookies on the devices of visitors located in the European Union or United Kingdom without their prior consent.

Our cookie consent mechanism will:

  • Present a clear notice on first visit explaining our use of cookies;
  • Offer granular consent options (accept all / essential only / customize);
  • Not use pre-ticked boxes or dark patterns to obtain consent;
  • Allow you to withdraw consent as easily as it was given;
  • Record and store your consent preferences.

Your consent preferences are stored in the cookie_consent first-party cookie for 12 months.
